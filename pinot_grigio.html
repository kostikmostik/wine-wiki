Pinot Grigio

Pinot gris, pinot grigio or Grauburgunder is a white wine grape variety of the species Vitis vinifera. By the way, the names “Pinot Gris” and “Pinot Grigio” are technically interchangeable. Pinot Gris means “gray Pinot” in French and Pinot Grigio is the Italian name. Pinot Grigio is growing in steep alpine valleys from Italy through Austria, Slovenia, and Hungary. The mountains are a powerful force on the agriculture there, and it ensures grapes maintain high acidity. The resulting wines are zippy and lean.
